What is Alternative Medicine?

Definition: Healthcare outside mainstream medicine
Used INSTEAD OF conventional treatment

  • Not taught in medical schools
  • Limited scientific validation
  • 30-41% of adults use CAM
Alternative: Used INSTEAD OF proven treatment

What is Complementary Medicine?

Definition: Non-mainstream used TOGETHER WITH conventional medicine

  • ‚úì Enhances conventional treatment
  • ‚úì Growing hospital acceptance
  • ‚úì Examples: Acupuncture for chemo nausea
Complementary: Used TOGETHER WITH proven treatment

What is Integrative Medicine?

Definition: Evidence-based combination of mainstream + CAM

  • ‚úì Patient-centered (body, mind, spirit)
  • ‚úì Evidence-based selections
  • ‚úì 60+ US academic centers
Integrative: Best of both systems based on evidence

Five CAM Categories

  • 1. Whole Systems: Ayurveda, TCM, Homeopathy
  • 2. Mind-Body: Yoga, Meditation, Tai Chi
  • 3. Biological: Herbs, Supplements
  • 4. Manipulative: Massage, Chiropractic
  • 5. Energy: Reiki (limited evidence)

Your Professional Responsibilities

  • 1. ASK about CAM use (non-judgmental)
  • 2. DOCUMENT in medical record
  • 3. KNOW drug interactions
  • 4. ASSESS if replacing necessary treatment
  • 5. EDUCATE about evidence
  • 6. INTEGRATE when appropriate
  • 7. REFER to qualified practitioners
  • 8. MONITOR effectiveness

7-Question Appraisal Framework

  1. Evidence? (RCTs, systematic review?)
  2. Mechanism? (Plausible vs. implausible?)
  3. Harms? (Toxicity, interactions?)
  4. Complement/Replace? (Critical for safety)
  5. Practitioner qualified? (Licensed?)
  6. Patient informed? (Understands risks?)
  7. Monitoring? (Follow-up plan?)
